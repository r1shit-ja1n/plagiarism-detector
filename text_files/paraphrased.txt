Assignment: Reading Comprehension and Analysis - "1984"

Q1: Discuss the primary function of the telescreens in the novel.
In Oceania, telescreens have two main functions: pushing non-stop government propaganda and spying on the citizens. Because they work both ways, people are never in private, creating a constant feeling of fear and forcing total obedience.

Q2: How does the concept of "Newspeak" control thought?
The goal of Newspeak is to shrink the capacity for human thinking. The government removes vocabulary connected to liberty and independence to ensure that committing thoughtcrime is completely impossible, as the words needed to even imagine a revolt are erased.

Q3: What does the glass paperweight symbolize for Winston?
To Winston, the old paperweight acts as a physical link to history prior to the Party taking over. It stands for a delicate, beautiful piece of objective truth that he wishes to live inside, though it gets destroyed by the authorities in the end.

Q4: Analyze the paradox of the Party's slogan: "Ignorance is Strength."
The phrase implies that the ruling class gains power from the lack of knowledge among the common people. When the public remains uneducated and blind to their subjugation, the government guarantees its rule is never threatened.

Q5: Contrast Winston's rebellion with Julia's rebellion.
Winston fights back on an intellectual and ideological level, wanting to figure out why the government changes the past to destroy them. On the other hand, Julia’s resistance is physical and self-centered. She violates the law for personal enjoyment and doesn't care much about overthrowing the regime.

Q6: What is the significance of the "Two Minutes Hate"?
This daily event is a mind-control method meant to bring people together using mutual anger. It takes the citizens' hidden sadness and frustration and points it at an outside enemy, stopping them from blaming the government for their miserable lives.

Q7: Explain the concept of "Doublethink."
Doublethink refers to the mental ability to believe in two opposing ideas at the exact same time. Party members need this skill so they can accept the government's lies as facts while ignoring what they personally remember to be true.

Q8: Why does the Party constantly rewrite history?
The government constantly edits history so they always look perfect and never wrong. By making sure their old forecasts are flawless, people have no proof to challenge the Party. Controlling history allows them to dominate the future.

Q9: What role do the "proles" play in Winston's hope for the future?
Winston feels that the only chance to defeat the government comes from the working class. They constitute most of society and are mostly ignored by the authorities, meaning they keep their humanity and have the massive population needed to rebel.

Q10: What is the ultimate purpose of Room 101?
This room isn't just for physical pain; it exists to crush an individual by using their deepest, most specific phobia against them. The main objective is to make the person turn against their loved ones, wiping out their soul and filling the void with true devotion to Big Brother.
